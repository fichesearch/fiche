To keep results useful, Fiche filters out spammy and commercial websites by default.

If something isn't showing up — like YouTube, Amazon, Reddit, etc. — mention it in your search.

This also applies to images and video, which are filtered out to prioritize text-based results.

To search for images, click the "Images" toggle (▼) below the search bar.

To search for video, just include words like “video,” “clip,” or mention a platform directly.

If results seem off, tap the "Report bad search" button so it can be fixed.

How This Works

Fiche is not a search engine. It's many filters that work to change how Google operates.

Its intentions are to block AI slop, SEO spam, clickbait listicles, outrage farming, corporate funnels, obtrusive UI, obnoxious video noise, and pay-to-play boosts, while resurfacing what used to be easy to find: niche websites, personal blogs, and actual relevant information from actual human beings.

Just trying to make Google a useful search engine again in this dying mall of an internet (while barely knowing what I'm doing as a first time coder).

There are some (hopefully) fun ultra-specific filters that you can toggle on and off if you're anything like me and enjoy wasting time just looking stuff up.
